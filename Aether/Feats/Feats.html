Feats

A large part of Aether are its feats. These represent things your character has learned or achieved. At each level, the player chooses a feat for his / her character to learn / gain. If you want, you can give your character a feat, which he would not take himself (e.g. you can think of a story, where your character became cursed, and thus transforms slowly. Just give him one of the transormative feats, and the character expieriences the change he would object to).

With something similar in mind, only very few feats represent the origin of learning / 'acquisition' process. The player can think of it himself (maybe he became more proficient with a weapon over time, maybe he got a special training session at the towns best swordsman etc.) or ask the GM for some advice.

It might happen, that some feats make the story, in which the character is playing, significantly less interesting. If this might be the case, ask your GM for permission to take the feat (e.g. a feat, which lets you determine the cardinal points might be problematic in a world without good (constant) reference points).

A feat has three features. A name, an effect and a prerequisite. The name is used to reference the feat, the effect describes what it does (if there are any questions, on how exactly they work, think about, how the feat is intended to work), while the prerequisite gives certain characteristics, which have to be met in order to take the feat. These consist most often of ability score requirements and maybe some other feat, which has to be taken in advance. There are some special signs used in some feats:

'A, B': A and B have to be met in order to take this feat.

'A / B': Either A or B has to be met. You dont get any bonus from meeting both.

'A + B X': The sum of A and B has to reach the number X (e.g. Strength + Dexterity 18 is met by both Str 9, Dex 9 aswell as Str 12, Dex 6).

Some feats can be taken more than once (this will always be indicated!). If a feat can only be taken a certain number of times, it will be indicated. For these feats, there is an additional sign:

'A X (+Y)' (e.g. Dexterity 12 (+6)): To take the feat for the first time, you have to have X in A (12 Dex). If you want to take it another time, you have to have X+Y in A (18 Dex). For each additional time you want to take it, add Y in the prerequisite (24, 30 and so on).
