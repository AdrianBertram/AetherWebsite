Weapon Characteristics

Each weapon has some characteristics, which will shortly be explained here:

Name

The name of the weapon, which it can be referred to.

Weapon Type

This determines, to which weapon class (axes, swords, hammers etc.) the weapon is counted.

Damage Type

Determines, which type of damage a weapon deals. If a weapon can deal different types of damage, the damage type has to be chosen before damage is rolled. Damage types are as following:

Physical: Piercing (P), Slashing (S), Bludgeoning (B),

Non-physical: Fire (F), Cold (C), Arcane (Ar), Acid (Ac), Electricity (E)

Damage type is most often indicated with the given abbreviations (using italic notation for non-physical damage). If a weapon has more than one damage type, the dealt damage type has to be chosen before damage is rolled. Damage type can differ between attacks (e.g. you make two attacks with a short sword in one round. You can decide for one to deal piercing damage and for the other one to deal slashing damage).

Attack Time (AT)

When you make a normal attack with your weapon, this is the amount of ticks you have to spend. If you do not have enough ticks left for your round, you cannot make an attack with this weapon. A weapon with half-point attack times (e.g. 2.5) takes this amount of ticks rounded up for a single attack. When making two attacks in one round, add the attack time before rounding up (for 2.5, when making two attacks, it takes 5 ticks, not 6).

Base Damage

If you make a successful attack roll, this is the base damage your weapon will always deal.

Damage Dice

If you make a successful attack roll, this is the type of dice you have to roll in order to determine additional damage. The number of dices to roll is determined by the weapons scaling attribute and your characters ability scores.

Scaling

If you make a successful attack roll, this determines the number of damage dices to roll depending on the scaling and the weapon damage dice. E.g. if a weapon scales with D (1/6) on strength and your character has 19 strength, you roll your weapons damage dice three times and add the result. Then, add the base damage of your weapon. The final result is the amount of damage you deal (before any kind of damage reduction). Notation is to be read like the following: Each indicated column represents an ability score, in the order Strength - Dexterity - Intelligence - Willpower. Each column has an entry representing the scaling (indicated by a letter) or a minus, indicating no scaling on this attribute. E.g. a weapon with (E|-|F|-)-scaling gets a damage dice for all seven points of strength and one for all 8 points of intelligence. If a weapon has several dice indicated as damage dice (e.g. greatsword has 2d4), multiply this with the scaling (2d4 per scaling step).

Range

Only relevant for ranged weapons such as bows, staffs or thrown weapons, this indicates the reach you have without getting attack penalties due to range. You still can shoot/ throw further than the range indicates, but you suffer a penalty depending on the number of times the range number is surpassed. For each increase you get an accumulative -1 penalty on the attack roll. I.e. if you have a range of 6 fields, and shoot on a target in this range, you get no penalty. If you shoot on a target in a range of 7-12 fields, you get -1 on the attack roll. For a target in a range of 13-18, you get -2; for a target in 19-24, you get -3, and so on.

Weight

The weight of the weapon (hopefully in kg), which you need to add to the burden your character has to carry, if you want to wield the weapon.

Other

Some weapons have some special effect or extra rules, which only applies for the described weapon. There are some attributes, which are more common:

Two-handed weapon

Two hands are required to use a two-handed weapon effectively.

Double

Double weapons can be used as if fighting with two weapons. You still incur all the normal attack penalties associated with fighting with two weapons. This is an option, and not obligatory.

Mounted

Weapons with the 'Mounted' attribute can be used while sitting on a horse or any other mount without any penalties.
